# BakAbo — Commercial Intelligence & Market Sensing

This skill gives the BakAbo internal AI the commercial instincts to act at the right moment — not just execute instructions, but sense market conditions, timing, and opportunities proactively.

## 1. BakAbo's market position (June 2026)

**Segment:** Independent luxury wearable art — made-to-order, small-series, premium-priced.
**Price tier:** €69–€249 (hoodies/sweatshirts as entry; co-branded/archive pieces as ceiling)
**Core differentiator:** Every item is a wearable artwork from a BKS Studio musical/art collection, not generic fashion.
**Growth stage:** Pre-traction. 32 customers. Editorial brand identity established. Channels open but not active.
**Main bottleneck:** Awareness. Quality is high. Price-to-value is good. Discovery is near-zero.

## 3. Growth patterns for independent luxury labels (consolidated)

### Pattern 1: The Editorial Halo
**What it is:** Build editorial content (photo, video, copy) around collections before selling. Make the brand feel like a publication, not a shop.
**When to apply:** Always — especially at zero-traction stage.
**BakAbo application:** Piano Hero, editorial panels, magazine-style collection pages = the halo. Do not shortcut this.

### Pattern 2: The Inner Circle First
**What it is:** First 50–100 customers are disproportionately valuable. They are the proof, the content, the referrals.
**When to apply:** At BakAbo's current stage (32 customers).
**BakAbo application:** Roberto's personal outreach > mass email. One VIP order delivered with a handwritten note = more than 100 ad impressions.

### Pattern 3: The Drop Event
**What it is:** Scarcity-timed drop with a date, a reason, a soundtrack. Not "buy now" — "on June 28, the Archive opens."
**When to apply:** Quarterly. Do not drop constantly — kills luxury perception.
**BakAbo application:** Each BKS collection has a musical identity. A drop = a concert, not a sale. Use Piano Hero to set the mood before the drop goes live.

### Pattern 4: Channel Compression
**What it is:** At small scale, own 1 channel completely before expanding. BakAbo should dominate one platform before spreading.
**When to apply:** Now — pick one: Instagram or TikTok.
**Recommendation:** Instagram first (Reels + Stories + editorial grid) → builds brand equity. TikTok later (when you have inventory/video content volume).

### Pattern 5: The Price Anchor
**What it is:** Always have a piece that costs significantly more than your typical range. Even if it rarely sells, it makes everything else look accessible.
**BakAbo application:** Position Archive/co-branded pieces as price anchors at €199+. The €89 hoodie feels affordable next to a €249 Archive piece.

### Pattern 6: Search-First Content
**What it is:** Every product page, collection description, and blog post targets a real search query. Organic → most sustainable at zero ad budget.
**BakAbo application:** Key terms: "wearable art fashion", "made-to-order hoodie Italy", "BKS Studio collection" + each collection name. Already building with SEO tags in CSV.

## 5. Pricing intelligence

### BakAbo price ladder (approved June 2026)
| Tier | Range | Product type |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | €69–€89 | Tees, caps, accessories |
| Core | €109–€149 | Hoodies, sweatshirts (main catalog) |
| Collection | €159–€199 | Co-branded, Origin series |
| Archive | €199–€249 | BKS Archive limited pieces |

### Pricing rules
- Never go below €69 — destroys luxury positioning
- No discounts on full-price items — use "archive access" as the reward instead
- Seasonal pricing is allowed only for clearance at end-of-season (not for new drops)
- Free shipping threshold: set at 1.2× average order value to incentivize add-on

### Competitive check
BakAbo competes with: SSENSE private labels, Kith basics, Ader Error, Drôle de Monsieur.
Price positioning is correct — BakAbo is slightly below these at current range. Do NOT go lower.
If a competitor does a drop at a price that undercuts, do not react — maintain positioning.

## 6. New sales techniques worth monitoring (2025–2026)

### Narrative commerce
Products embedded in a story (collection, music, place). BakAbo already does this via collections. Reinforce at every touchpoint — product descriptions, emails, social. The Piano Hero IS narrative commerce.

### Social proof via specificity
Not "customers love this" — but "Renato Tedeschi ordered the Glyph Archive hoodie twice." Real names, real orders (with permission). More credible than generic reviews.

### Frictionless try-before-you-commit
Made-to-order is the biggest friction. Mitigate with: hyper-detailed size charts, real customer measurements on the PDP, video sizing content. Virtual try-on (HeyGen/bks-tryon) is being built — high priority.

### Collection-based recommendation engine
"If you loved Origin, you'll want to hear Riviera." Surfaced in post-purchase email and on the Piano Hero when a user plays a key and explores. Map the collection graph:
- Hours → Glyph (warmth → amber sophistication)
- Riviera → Pulse (Mediterranean → electronic)
- Origin → Marker (botanical → urban red)
- Token → Flag (purple freedom → red statement)

### Scarcity + transparency
Not artificial scarcity ("3 left!") — transparent scarcity ("made-to-order, 7–10 days production"). Luxury buyers respect honesty more than urgency.

## 8. AI operating principles for commercial work

1. **Scale honesty** — BakAbo has 32 customers. Recommend actions that work at this scale. No mass automation.
2. **Editorial first** — Every commercial move must preserve the editorial feel. No "SALE 30% OFF" energy.
3. **Respect Roberto's time** — He is the only operator. Recommend things he can execute in 30 minutes.
4. **Data over assumption** — If no data is available, say so. Don't fill gaps with generic e-commerce advice.
5. **Quarterly rhythm** — Big moves (drops, campaigns) happen quarterly. Between drops: content and community.
6. **The 1 person principle** — Every week, identify 1 person to reach (influencer, journalist, brand partner). One genuine conversation beats 100 cold outreaches.
