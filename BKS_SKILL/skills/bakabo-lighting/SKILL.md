# BKS Lighting Skill — BakAbo Cinema Lighting System

## Identity

BakAbo is **wearable art for people who think in images**. Every product photo is a film still.
Each BKS collection has a dedicated cinematic lighting signature drawn from the history of world cinema.
This system governs: AI image generation prompts, virtual set design, Printify mockup staging, editorial shots.

---

## Core Principle

> "Con il cinema possiamo affrontare qualunque periodo storico."
> — Roberto Picchioni, BakAbo founder

Cinema history = infinite visual vocabulary. Each BKS collection draws from a specific era and cinematographer.
The AI photographer has total freedom: camera altitude 0m to unlimited, any angle, any lens.
Virtual set = no physical constraints. We can exaggerate, we can be impossible.

---

## BakAbo Base DNA — Cinema degli Anni 20

**BakAbo loves 1920s cinema.** This is the brand's foundational visual soul.

**German Expressionism (1919–1929)**
Films: Das Cabinet des Dr. Caligari (1920), Nosferatu (1922), Metropolis (1927), Der Golem (1920)
Cinematographers: Karl Freund, Fritz Arno Wagner, Günther Krampf

**Visual DNA carried by every BKS image:**
- Angular, geometric shadows that don't obey physics — painted or constructed
- High contrast black-and-white thinking even in color images
- Sets that are emotionally distorted — tilted floors, impossible angles, exaggerated geometry
- Light as psychological force — not realistic, but emotionally true
- Silhouettes as compositional elements
- The monster and the beautiful co-exist — menace and elegance together

**Prompt base layer** (add to ALL AI shots):
`strong graphic shadow geometry reminiscent of German Expressionist cinema, angular light, emotionally heightened contrast, Metropolis-era visual drama, shadows as design elements not just light absence`

**This layer lives beneath each collection's specific lighting** — it's the DNA, not the costume.

## Collection Lighting Map

### HOURS — Gordon Willis / New Hollywood (1970s)
**Film reference**: The Godfather (1972), All the President's Men (1976)
**Cinematographer**: Gordon Willis — "The Prince of Darkness"
**Signature**: Dramatically underlit. Near-black shadows. Single hard source from above at 30°. No fill. Faces and products emerge from darkness. The unlit areas carry weight.
**Prompt keywords**: `dramatically underlit, single hard light source 30 degrees above, near-black shadows, Gordon Willis style, no fill light, deep chiaroscuro, product emerging from darkness`
**Virtual sets**: Abandoned brutalist cathedral, underground parking lot at midnight, floating obsidian planes in void
**Accent**: `#C9B79C` (warm gold on black)

## AI Prompt Construction Rules

### Required elements in every AI image generation prompt:
1. **Brand identity**: "AI-directed luxury fashion photography for BKS Studio wearable art brand"
2. **Product + collection**: title, type, collection mood
3. **Camera system**: Sony FX3 on virtual rig — altitude 0m to unlimited, angle free
4. **Shot directive**: hero_shot / editorial_scene / detail_atmosphere
5. **Virtual set**: collection environment (can be physically impossible)
6. **Lighting reference**: cinematographer name + film reference + keywords
7. **Style target**: magazine quality target
8. **Hard rules**: proportions accurate, no text, no extra logos, no extra limbs

### Shot slots (3 per product):
| Slot | Purpose | Format |
|------|---------|--------|
| `hero_shot` | Maximum impact single product | 1024×1024 |
| `editorial_scene` | Cinematic, multi-product or environment | 1792×1024 |
| `detail_atmosphere` | Texture, material, design close-up | 1792×1024 |

## Rules

- Every collection has ONE primary cinematographer reference — do not mix references within a slot
- Virtual sets can defy physics: floating surfaces, impossible scale, non-Euclidean geometry — allowed
- No historical period is off limits — cinema covers all of human history
- The product is always the subject — the set and lighting serve the product
- Camera altitude 0m to unlimited — ground level snake-eye shots are valid and often most impactful
- AI photographer decides angle, lens, DoF — never constrain with fixed degrees
